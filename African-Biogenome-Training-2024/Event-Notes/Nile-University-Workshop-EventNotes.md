**Lecture 1: Biodiversity Genomics in the '-Omics' Era**   
*Dr. Abdollah Sharaf*     

**Notes:**

* Biodiversity genomics studies biological variation from DNA to ecosystems.
* Key applications: species identification, phylogenetics, conservation, drug discovery, bioprospecting, neodomestication, and de-extinction.
* High-throughput sequencing technologies (NGS, PacBio, Oxford Nanopore) enable detailed genomic analyses.
* Ethical and ecological considerations are essential when attempting de-extinction or neodomestication.
* Planning genome sequencing experiments involves careful sample collection, library prep, sequencing, and data analysis.

**Key Takeaway:**

* Understanding genomics workflows enhances ability to design conservation and functional genomics projects.
* Inspired to consider genomic data integration for molecular biomarker discovery in research projects.

---

**Lecture 2: Biodiversity Loss in Marine Biology**       
*Dr. Mohamed E. Hassan*

**Notes:**

* Overview of marine biodiversity decline and human impact.
* Integration of genomic, metagenomic, and environmental datasets is critical to monitor ecosystems.
* DNA barcoding and eDNA enable species detection without direct sampling.

**Key Takeaway:**

* Bioinformatics approaches in marine biology can inform conservation strategies; methods are applicable to analyzing complex biological datasets in my cancer genomics research.

---

**Lecture 3: Comparison Between Long-Read and Short-Read Sequencing Technology**       
*Dr. Mohamed E. Hassan*

**Notes:**

* Short-read sequencing (Illumina): high accuracy, limited read length.
* Long-read sequencing (PacBio, Nanopore): resolves structural variants, repetitive regions, and complex genomes.
* Combining both approaches can optimize genome assembly.

**Notes:**
